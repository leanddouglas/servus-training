**NYLON** (Blue theme: `#1565c0`)
- *Subtitle:* Most common residential fiber
- *Flame:* Blue base with orange tip — steady, controlled burn. The flame has a distinct two-tone color: cool blue at the base transitioning to warm orange at the tip.
- *Smoke:* White to gray, moderate amount. Thin wisping smoke trails, not heavy.
- *Odor:* **Celery or sealing wax** — ILLUSTRATE as celery stalks with leaves
- *Residue:* Hard, round gray/brown bead — ILLUSTRATE as a 3D sphere with realistic shadow, gradient from tan to brown, with a subtle highlight/reflection
- *Memory Trick:* "Nylon = Celery Stick" — the unique celery smell is your #1 identifier
- *pH Range:* 2–10

---

**POLYESTER** (Purple theme: `#8e24aa`)
- *Subtitle:* Oil-loving, stain-resistant
- *Flame:* Burns rapidly, **sputters and sparks** — show an aggressive flame with small spark particles flying outward from the flame
- *Smoke:* **HEAVY BLACK SMOKE** — this is the key identifier. Illustrate as thick, dark, billowing smoke clouds. Make the smoke visually dominant and clearly darker than all other fibers.
- *Odor:* **Sweet, overripe fruit** — ILLUSTRATE as a bruised/overripe apple with brown spots and faint stink lines
- *Residue:* Shiny, hard BLACK bead — ILLUSTRATE as a glossy black sphere with a bright white specular reflection highlight (shinier than nylon's bead)
- *Memory Trick:* "Polyester = Rotten Apple" — sweet smell + black smoke = polyester every time
- *pH Range:* 4–10

---

**OLEFIN / Polypropylene** (Orange theme: `#e65100`)
- *Subtitle:* Solution-dyed, bleach-safe
- *Flame:* Burns rapidly, **melts and drips like candle wax** — show the flame with melted material dripping downward in droplets below
- *Smoke:* Light smoke — very minimal, thin wisps
- *Odor:* **Asphalt or burning candles** — ILLUSTRATE as a lit candle sitting on a dark asphalt road surface (combine both smell associations in one image)
- *Residue:* Hard, round, light-colored/tan bead — ILLUSTRATE as a lighter-toned 3D sphere, cream/tan color with soft shadow
- *Memory Trick:* "Olefin = Road Candle" — smells like hot asphalt + candle wax, melts like wax
- *pH Range:* 2–12 (bleach-safe!)

---

**WOOL** (Green theme: `#2e7d32`)
- *Subtitle:* Natural protein fiber — pH sensitive!
- *Flame:* **Sizzles and self-extinguishes** — does NOT sustain flame. Show small spark lines radiating outward (like a sparkler) and a red X symbol indicating the flame dies
- *Smoke:* Very light gray — barely visible, thinnest of all four fibers
- *Odor:* **Burning hair or feathers** — ILLUSTRATE as hair strands with glowing/ember tips, since wool IS a protein fiber (animal hair)
- *Residue:* **Black crumbly ash that crushes to powder** — NOT a bead. ILLUSTRATE as a scattered pile of irregular black chunks with small powder particles spreading outward. Show a downward arrow suggesting "crushable"
- *Memory Trick:* "Wool = Barbershop Floor" — it's hair! Smells like hair because it IS hair (protein fiber)
- *pH Range:* **4–8 ONLY** — most restricted! Style this bar with a warning/red color to emphasize danger

## CRITICAL VISUAL DISTINCTIONS TO EMPHASIZE
The whole point of this guide is helping technicians **tell fibers apart**. Make these differences visually OBVIOUS:

| Feature | Nylon | Polyester | Olefin | Wool |
|---------|-------|-----------|--------|------|
| Flame color | Blue+Orange (two-tone) | Orange (sputtering) | Orange (melting) | Orange (dying) |
| Smoke amount | Medium gray | **HEAVY BLACK** | Light | Very light |
| Residue type | Hard gray bead | Hard BLACK shiny bead | Hard light bead | **Crumbly ash (NO bead)** |
| Key behavior | Steady burn | Sputters/sparks | Melts/drips | Self-extinguishes |

The **smoke illustration for polyester** must be dramatically darker and heavier than all others.
The **residue for wool** must look completely different from the other three (ash vs. bead).
The **wool flame** must clearly show it goes OUT (self-extinguishes).
