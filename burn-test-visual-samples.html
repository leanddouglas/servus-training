Burn Test Visual Concepts

3 different approaches — pick the one that clicks best for your students

CONCEPT A — "Smell Memory Cards"

Each fiber gets a color + emoji + memorable smell tagline. Students remember "Nylon = celery" or "Polyester = rotten fruit" — the smell is the anchor that sticks in memory. Great for quick field reference.

🥬
Nylon
"Celery & Sealing Wax"
🔥 Flame: Blue base, orange tip
💨 Smoke: White/gray, moderate
👃 Smell: Celery or sealing wax
⚫ Residue: Hard, round gray/brown bead
⚠️ pH: Safe range 2-10
🍎
Polyester
"Sweet Overripe Fruit"
🔥 Flame: Burns rapidly, sputters
💨 Smoke: Heavy black smoke
👃 Smell: Sweet, overripe fruit
⚫ Residue: Shiny, hard black bead
⚠️ pH: Safe range 4-10
🕯️
Olefin
"Asphalt & Candle Wax"
🔥 Flame: Burns rapidly, melts fast
💨 Smoke: Light smoke
👃 Smell: Asphalt or burning candles
⚫ Residue: Hard, round, light bead
⚠️ pH: Safe range 2-12 (bleach-safe)
💇
Wool
"Burning Hair / Feathers"
🔥 Flame: Sizzles, self-extinguishes
💨 Smoke: Light gray
👃 Smell: Burning hair or feathers
⚫ Residue: Black, crumbly ash (crushable)
⚠️ pH: Safe range 4-8 ONLY
CONCEPT B — "Side-by-Side Comparison"

A single table where students compare all 4 fibers at once — same test, different results. The brain learns by contrast. When they smell something in the field, they scan across the row and match it. Printable for the van clipboard.

CONCEPT C — "Step Flow + Decision Cards"

Shows the process first (3 steps), then the diagnosis (4 result cards). Each card has a bold memory trick at the bottom. Combines the flow of your original with cleaner card layout. Best for students who think in sequences.

1
✂️
Isolate Sample
Pull a small tuft from a hidden area (closet corner, under furniture). Use tweezers — never cut loop pile.
2
🔥
Ignite & Observe
Use odorless butane lighter (never matches). Hold tuft to flame. Watch for: melt vs. sizzle vs. burn.
3
🔍
Check 4 Pillars
Document: Flame behavior, Smoke color, Odor, and Residue (ash or bead).
