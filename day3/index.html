MODULE 1: Hot Water Extraction — The Gold Standard Method

How HWE Works

Hot water extractionMost powerful carpet cleaning method using hot water and vacuum (HWE) is the most powerful carpet cleaning method. It injects hot water and cleaning solution deep into carpet fibers, then vacuums everything back out — water, solution, and dissolved soil.

Three things make HWE work:

  • Heat: Opens fiber structure, breaks down oil and grease bonds
  • Pressure: Drives solution into the pile
  • Vacuum (CFM)Cubic feet per minute — suction strength: Pulls out water and soil. High CFM = faster drying

The Industry Standard: IICRC recommends HWE as the primary method for residential carpet. Servus uses HWE as the gold standard for deep cleans.

The Wand: Your Control Tool

The wand controls everything — pressure, spray pattern, vacuum, and pace. It is your primary tool for precise carpet cleaning.

Wand Techniques & Patterns +
Key Techniques:
  • Push stroke: Apply solution (valve open) — solution sprays into carpet
  • Pull stroke: Extract (vacuum on) — pull water and soil out
  • Heavy soil: Apply chemical on BOTH push and pull strokes
  • Wand position: Keep FLAT on surface at all times while valve is open
  • Never leave stationary: Continuous motion prevents over-saturation
Overlap Rule:

Each pass should overlap the previous by 50%. This prevents streaks and ensures full coverage.

Pass Speed:

Slow and steady. Rushing = less extraction = wetter carpet = browning risk.

Directions:

For heavily soiled carpet, clean in 2 directions (North/South, then East/West) — same pattern as Whittaker TRIO.

Spray Pattern Check:

Before starting, hold wand 12 inches above floor and open valve. You should see a full, even fan spray from the nozzle. Adjust or replace nozzle if pattern is uneven.

MODULE 2: Encapsulation — The Whittaker Smart Care TRIO

How Encapsulation Works

EncapsulationLow-moisture cleaning using crystallizing polymers is a low-moisture cleaning method. It uses a special crystallizing polymer solution that surrounds soil particles, then dries into brittle crystals. Those crystals are vacuumed away at the next scheduled vacuum — no water extraction needed.

Why it works:

  • Low moisture = faster drying (as little as 30 minutes)
  • No re-wetting of backing or subfloor
  • Ideal for commercial loop pile, interim maintenance, and moisture-sensitive areas
  • Crystallized residue does NOT re-attract soil (unlike detergent residue)

MODULE 3: Bonnet Cleaning

How Bonnet Cleaning Works

Bonnet cleaning uses a floor machine (rotary buffer) with an absorbent pad (the "bonnet") to clean carpet surface soil. Solution is sprayed on carpet, then the spinning bonnet absorbs surface dirt.

Important: This is a surface-only method — it does NOT penetrate deep into the pile.

When to use bonnet:

  • Quick maintenance clean between deep cleans
  • Stair cleaning (wand attachment)
  • Areas where equipment access is limited
  • Post-event spot freshening

When NOT to use bonnet:

  • First-time cleans
  • Heavy soil
  • Any carpet with embedded soil in the base

Product: Proxi Spray and Walk Away (RTU, encapsulation-compatible) — spray onto carpet before bonneting.

MODULE 4: Critical Equipment Parameters

Understanding PSIPounds per Square Inch — measures water pressure from the pump. Higher PSI breaks down soil faster. and CFMCubic Feet per Minute — measures suction/airflow strength. Higher CFM removes more moisture and prevents wicking.

Two numbers define cleaning power: PSIPounds per Square Inch — the pressure that drives solution into carpet fibers and CFMCubic Feet per Minute — the airflow that pulls moisture out of carpet.

PSI (Pressure): Higher PSI means stronger water spray. This breaks down soil faster. Truck-mounts reach 200+ PSI. The Warrior 500 portable delivers up to 500 PSI (adjustable).

CFM (Airflow/Suction): Higher CFM means more moisture removalThe amount of water pulled out of carpet during extraction — critical for preventing wicking and browning. This prevents wickingWhen moisture deep in carpet backing rises to the surface as it dries, bringing soil with it — causes brown lines or spots to reappear after cleaning. Truck-mounts pull 300–400 CFM. Portables like the Warrior 500 pull 150–200 CFM.

Normal (Regular) 150–180 150–250 5–10 min
Heavy (First-Time) 180–200+ 250–350+ 10–15 min

MODULE 6: Rinse & Neutralization by Method

HWE: Acid Rinse Is Mandatory

After any alkaline prespray, carpet pH is elevated (9–12). You MUST bring it back to neutral (4.5–5.5) with an acid rinseAcidic solution used after alkaline cleaning. This prevents browning and rapid resoiling.

  • All Fiber Rinse (pH 4–4.5, dilution 1:6): Standard acid rinse for all HWE work.
  • Fibre Rinse (pH 2.3): Heavy residue, stubborn browning situations.

The Squeaky Clean Test: Rub a damp white cloth on cleaned carpet. If it squeaks and stays white — pH is neutral. If it's sticky or discolors — residue remains.

Encapsulation: No Rinse Required

EncapsulationLow-moisture cleaning method solution crystallizes as it dries. There is no residue to rinse. This is a major advantage of the TRIO system for commercial maintenance.

Servus Standard in Action

If carpet dries sticky after HWE cleaning, the acid rinse was skipped or insufficient. This is the #1 cause of rapid resoiling after professional cleaning. Sticky = residue = re-attracts soil.

MODULE 7: Drying & Post-Cleaning by Method

HWE Drying (Warrior 500)

The Warrior 500 leaves more moisture than a truck-mount. Proper drying procedure is critical.

After extracting:

  1. Run dry strokes (vacuum only, no spray) — minimum 2 extra passes
  2. Set up air movers: 1 mover per 200 sq ft
  3. Drying time: 12–24 hours (depending on humidity and airflow)
  4. If possible: open windows or run HVAC to increase air circulation
  5. Never let client walk on wet carpet without shoe covers — tracking-in soil immediately

Warrior 500 Service Schedule:

  • After EACH use: Empty solution tank, clean filter screen, flush with 1–2 gallons clean water, rinse recovery tank
  • Twice monthly: Flush with white vinegar solution (1 qt vinegar to 2 gal water) to prevent alkaline buildup in system
  • Monthly: Lubricate pressure regulator u-cup with Superlube
  • Quarterly: Check vacuum motor carbon brushes — replace when less than 3/8" (9.5mm) long

Encapsulation Drying (Whittaker TRIO)

Drying is minimal — Proxi Encap leaves very low moisture.

  • Carpet is ready to use in as little as 30 minutes
  • Ready to vacuum in 30 minutes (no need to wait — can vacuum immediately if needed)
  • No air movers typically needed for encapsulation
  • Dirt crystals remain locked in dried encapsulant until vacuumed

MODULE 10: Equipment Maintenance & Troubleshooting

Whittaker Smart Care TRIO — Maintenance

Daily & Ongoing Maintenance +
During Operation:
  • Rotate all 3 brushes end-to-end every 2–3 hours of operation (prolongs brush life, prevents gear damage)
  • Do NOT lubricate axles — keep clean and dry
  • NEVER rest machine with brushes directly on floor
  • Maximum 2 passes per direction — more passes damage fibers
After Each Use (TRIO):
  1. Unplug power cord at wall outlet
  2. Remove, empty and rinse solution tank (fresh water)
  3. Remove, empty and rinse collection hopper
  4. Tilt machine backward onto handle
  5. Push axles sideways and out to remove brushes (no tools needed if kept clean)
  6. Inspect brushes for wear — use Brush Wear Indicator hole on axle end to measure bristle length
  7. Wipe underside of machine with damp cloth
  8. Rotate front brush end-to-end before replacing, then rotate other 2 brushes
