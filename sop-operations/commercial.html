01

Purpose

This SOP defines the standard workflow for recurring commercial carpet maintenance programs. It covers both interim maintenance cleaning (Whittaker TRIO / encapsulation) and scheduled deep cleaning (HWE), ensuring consistent service across multi-area commercial facilities.

02

Scope

Applies to:

  • Office buildings, medical/dental offices, retail spaces, educational facilities
  • Recurring maintenance contracts (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, quarterly)
  • Both interim maintenance and deep extraction cleaning
  • Excludes: one-time residential jobs (see Residential SOP), emergency water damage, specialty restoration

Key Definitions:

  • Interim Maintenance — Low-moisture cleaning using Whittaker TRIO (encapsulation) for regular upkeep
  • Deep Clean (HWE) — Full hot water extraction for quarterly/semi-annual deep cleaning
  • Traffic Lanes — High-use areas requiring more frequent cleaning attention
  • Maintenance Schedule — Client-specific cleaning frequency plan (varies by contract)
03

Workflow Steps

1

Schedule & Access Coordination

Review maintenance schedule in CRM — confirm cleaning type (interim vs deep).

Coordinate building access (key fob, security code, building manager contact).

Confirm after-hours procedures if applicable (alarm codes, lock-up protocol).

Check client's area map for priority zones and access restrictions.

Crew Leader Confirms schedule, coordinates building access, reviews priorities

Crew Member Verifies equipment is appropriate for cleaning type (TRIO vs truckmount)

2

Site Arrival & Security

Sign in at building security/front desk (if applicable).

Follow building-specific access protocols (elevators, restricted areas).

Set up cleaning signage: "Cleaning in Progress" barriers at work areas.

After-hours: verify alarm system is set to "cleaning mode" or disabled per protocol.

Crew Leader Handles security sign-in, confirms access protocol

Crew Member Sets up signage and barriers

3

Area Assessment

Walk all scheduled areas — note soil levels, new stains, damage since last visit.

Compare against cleaning map/log from previous visit.

Photograph any new damage or concerns for documentation.

Determine if scheduled cleaning type is sufficient or if escalation is needed.

Crew Leader Assesses all areas, documents changes, decides on cleaning escalation

Crew Member Assists with photo documentation

4A

Interim Maintenance Cleaning (Whittaker TRIO)

Pre-vacuum all scheduled areas.

Apply encapsulation solution per manufacturer specs.

Run Whittaker TRIO in overlapping passes — ensure full coverage.

Focus extra passes on traffic lanes and soiled areas.

No rinse/extraction required — carpet is walk-ready immediately.

Crew Leader Monitors coverage quality, checks results

Crew Member Operates TRIO, performs pre-vacuum

4B

Deep Clean — HWE Extraction

Follow Steps 4–7 from Residential SOP (SOP-RES-001) with these commercial modifications:

Use commercial-grade pre-spray concentrations (typically stronger than residential).

Work in sections — complete one area fully before moving to next.

Coordinate with building schedule to allow drying time before occupancy.

Place air movers in completed areas immediately.

Crew Leader Manages section sequencing, coordinates with building contact for drying

Crew Member Performs extraction, moves air movers to completed sections

5

Multi-Area Tracking

Check off each area on the cleaning map as completed.

Note any areas skipped (locked offices, occupied rooms) — document reason.

Log soil level and treatment for each area.

If recurring issues appear in same zones → flag for client discussion.

Crew Leader Maintains area tracking log, flags recurring issues

Crew Member Reports completed areas to Crew Leader

6

Quality Verification

Spot-check completed areas under good lighting.

For interim cleaning: verify no visible residue, carpet has uniform appearance.

For deep clean: check for wand marks, remaining stains, over-wetting.

Take "after" photos of key areas (lobby, main corridors, conference rooms).

Crew Leader Performs quality spot-checks, approves completed areas

Crew Member Re-treats any flagged areas

7

Building Close-Out

Remove all equipment, signage, and barriers.

Ensure doors are locked, alarms are reset (after-hours protocol).

Sign out at security/front desk if applicable.

Leave area as found — move furniture back if repositioned.

Crew Leader Verifies building is secured, signs out

Crew Member Removes all equipment and signage

8

Documentation & Reporting

Complete maintenance visit log in CRM.

Upload photos (before/after for notable areas).

Note any areas requiring follow-up or escalation.

Update cleaning map with current soil assessments.

Submit maintenance report to building manager (per contract terms — weekly/monthly summary).

Crew Leader Completes all CRM documentation, submits reports

Crew Member Assists with photo uploads

9

Next Visit Preparation

Review upcoming schedule — note any special requests or contract changes.

Flag equipment needs (replacement pads for TRIO, chemical stock).

Communicate any building access changes to team.

Crew Leader Reviews next visit plan, communicates changes

Crew Member Prepares equipment and supplies for next scheduled visit

05

Safety

  • Follow all building-specific safety protocols (fire exits, evacuation routes)
  • Wet floor signage mandatory at all cleaning areas and pathways
  • After-hours: carry personal safety device, maintain contact with Office
  • Power cord management: tape down all cords crossing walkways
  • Chemical storage: never leave chemicals unattended in public areas
  • Emergency: know building emergency contact and nearest AED/first-aid location
After-hours solo work requires check-in with Office every 2 hours. If contact is lost, Office initiates safety protocol.
06

Systems & Tools

  • CRM/Scheduling: Maintenance schedules, visit logs, client history, reporting
  • Building Access: Key fobs, security codes, building manager contacts (stored securely in CRM)
  • Equipment — Interim: Whittaker TRIO, CRI-approved vacuum, encapsulation solution
  • Equipment — Deep Clean: Truckmount/portable extractor, pre-spray, air movers
  • Documentation: Area cleaning maps, photo capture, maintenance reports
  • Communication: Building manager contact, security desk, team chat

08

Quality Checks

  • Pre-vacuum completed before all wet cleaning
  • Interim cleaning: uniform appearance, no visible residue
  • Deep clean: no wand marks, proper extraction, no over-wetting
  • All scheduled areas cleaned (or skipped areas documented with reason)
  • Traffic lanes show visible improvement
  • Before/after photos for notable areas (lobby, main corridors)
  • Area tracking log complete and accurate
  • Building secured and alarm reset (after-hours)
  • Maintenance report filed within 24 hours
  • Equipment cleaned and restocked for next visit
09

Exceptions & Escalation

  • Locked/occupied area: Document as skipped, reschedule with building manager.
  • Soil level beyond maintenance cleaning: Flag for deep clean upgrade, notify Account Manager.
  • New damage discovered: Photo-document, report to Account Manager → building manager within 24 hours.
  • Equipment failure: Complete what's possible with backup equipment, reschedule remaining areas.
  • Building emergency (fire alarm, evacuation): Follow building emergency protocol, evacuate with all personnel, contact Office.
  • Access denied/security issue: Do not force entry, contact building manager and Office, document incident.
10

Close-Out

  1. Complete area tracking log — all zones accounted for
  2. Upload photos to CRM (minimum: before/after of main areas)
  3. Note exceptions, skipped areas, and follow-up items
  4. Submit visit report (CRM auto-generates for contract clients)
  5. Verify building security and lock-up procedures completed
  6. Equipment cleaned, TRIO pads replaced/rotated, chemicals restocked

01

Purpose

This SOP defines the standard workflow for emergency and add-on spot/stain treatment calls. It covers rapid-response triage, treatment selection, client communication, and documentation for both standalone emergency calls and add-on treatments during scheduled service.

02

Scope

Applies to:

  • Emergency stain calls (client requests urgent treatment within 24–48 hours)
  • Add-on spot treatments during scheduled residential or commercial service
  • Common scenarios: red wine spills, coffee, pet accidents, ink, food stains, tracked-in mud, blood
  • Excludes: flood/water damage (separate water damage SOP), permanent dye stains (set expectations), structural stains (mold, smoke damage — requires specialty treatment)

Key Definitions:

  • Spot — Surface-level discoloration that can typically be removed with proper treatment
  • Stain — Permanent or set-in discoloration that may only be improved, not fully removed
  • Emergency Call — Client requests treatment within 24–48 hours of incident
  • Add-On Treatment — Spot treatment performed during an already-scheduled service visit
  • Triage — Initial assessment to determine treatment approach and set expectations
03

Workflow Steps

1

Call Intake & Triage

Gather information: what was spilled, when it happened, what surface (carpet type if known), what the client has already done.

Critical: Has the client attempted DIY treatment? (Some home products cause permanent damage.)

Classify urgency: same-day response vs next-business-day.

For add-on treatments: assess during pre-inspection walk-through.

Crew Leader / Office Collects spill details, classifies urgency, schedules response

Important: "The sooner we treat it, the better the outcome. Fresh stains have significantly higher removal rates."
2

Arrival & Initial Assessment

Identify the stain type using FRP-009 (Spot vs Stain Identification).

Assess carpet fiber type (check label or use identification techniques from FRP-002).

Document with photos: size of affected area, color, current condition.

Test an inconspicuous area before applying any treatment.

Crew Leader Identifies stain type and fiber type, selects treatment approach

Crew Member Documents stain with photos, prepares testing area

3

Client Expectation Setting

Be honest about likely outcomes BEFORE starting treatment.

Use clear language:

  • "This is a spot — I'm confident we can remove it completely"
  • "This appears to be a permanent stain — we can improve it significantly but some discoloration may remain"
  • "The previous DIY treatment has set this in — we'll do our best but results may be limited"

Get verbal acknowledgment before proceeding.

Crew Leader Communicates expectations clearly, gets client acknowledgment

4

Treatment Selection

Select treatment based on stain type and fiber type (reference FRP-009 and FRP-003):

  • Protein stains (blood, food, dairy): Enzyme-based cleaner, cool/warm water — NEVER hot (sets protein)
  • Tannin stains (coffee, tea, wine): Oxidizing agent or tannin-specific spotter
  • Oil/grease stains (cooking oil, cosmetics): Solvent-based spotter, work from edges inward
  • Dye stains (red wine, fruit juice, ink): Red Relief protocol (FRP-010) for red dyes; specific spotters for ink
  • Pet stains (urine, feces, vomit): Enzyme treatment per FRP-011, check for padding contamination
  • Unknown stains: Start with mildest treatment, escalate as needed

Crew Leader Selects and prepares treatment chemicals

Crew Member Assists with chemical mixing per label instructions

5

Treatment Execution

Apply treatment following the specific protocol for the stain type.

Always work from the outside of the stain inward (prevents spreading).

Blot — never rub or scrub (fiber damage risk).

Allow proper dwell time for chemical to work.

For stubborn stains: repeat treatment up to 3 times before escalating.

Rinse treatment area thoroughly after each application.

Extract with HWE if available, or blot with clean white cloth.

Crew Leader Applies and monitors treatment, decides on repeat applications

Crew Member Assists with extraction and cleanup

6

Post-Treatment Assessment

Evaluate results against initial photos.

If stain remains after 3 treatment attempts:

  • Assess if further treatment could damage the fiber
  • Consider leaving remaining treatment for a follow-up visit (some stains continue to release over 24h)
  • Document final result with photos

Apply Scotchgard to treated area if client has protector service.

Crew Leader Evaluates results, decides on follow-up treatment

Crew Member Takes after photos, applies protector if applicable

7

Client Communication & Sign-Off

Show client the results, comparing before/after.

Explain what was done and any ongoing care instructions:

  • "Blot any new spills immediately — don't rub"
  • "This treated area may re-appear slightly as it dries — this is normal and will fade"
  • "If the stain reappears after drying, call us for a free re-treatment under warranty"

For stains that couldn't be fully removed: explain why and offer options (re-treatment, re-dyeing referral).

Collect sign-off.

Crew Leader Communicates results, provides care instructions, collects sign-off

8

Documentation & Follow-Up

Update CRM with: stain type, treatment used, results achieved, photos (before/after).

Flag any stains that may need follow-up treatment.

For warranty claims: set 7-day follow-up reminder in CRM.

For commercial clients: include in maintenance report.

Crew Leader Completes all documentation, sets follow-up reminders

Crew Member Assists with CRM photo uploads

04

Roles & Responsibilities

Role Key Responsibilities
Crew Leader Stain identification, treatment selection, client communication, quality decisions
Crew Member Documentation, chemical preparation, extraction assistance, equipment handling
Office / Dispatch Call intake, urgency triage, scheduling, follow-up reminders
05

Safety

  • Always wear chemical-resistant gloves when handling spotting agents
  • Eye protection required when mixing or applying solvent-based products
  • Ensure adequate ventilation when using solvent-based spotters in enclosed spaces
  • Never mix different chemical products — rinse thoroughly between applications
  • Check SDS for all chemicals used — know the first aid procedures
  • Bio-hazard protocol for bodily fluids: gloves + face protection, dispose of contaminated materials properly
If a client or crew member experiences a chemical reaction (skin irritation, breathing difficulty), flush the area, move to fresh air, and call 911 if symptoms persist.
06

Systems & Tools

  • Spotter Kit: Multi-chemical spot treatment kit with applicators
  • Chemicals: Enzyme cleaner, oxidizing agent, tannin spotter, solvent spotter, Red Relief solution
  • Equipment: Portable extractor (for standalone calls), white cotton cloths, spray bottles
  • Documentation: Phone camera for before/after, CRM mobile app
  • Reference: FRP-009 (Spot vs Stain ID), FRP-010 (Red Relief), FRP-011 (Pet Treatment), FRP-003 (pH Guide)

08

Quality Checks

  • Stain type correctly identified before treatment
  • Fiber type confirmed (to avoid chemical damage)
  • Inconspicuous area tested before full treatment
  • Client expectations set BEFORE treatment begins
  • Treatment applied from outside inward (no spreading)
  • Maximum 3 attempts before escalation decision
  • Before/after photos captured and uploaded
  • Client sign-off collected
  • Follow-up reminder set (if applicable)
  • CRM updated with full treatment details
09

Exceptions & Escalation

  • Unknown substance: Do not treat. Take samples/photos, consult with supervisor or chemical supplier. Safety first.
  • Previous DIY treatment caused damage: Document the damage, explain to client that prior treatment has limited what's achievable. Proceed with best available treatment.
  • Stain reappears after treatment: Schedule warranty re-treatment (free within 7 days per Servus policy). May require sub-surface treatment.
  • Fiber damage risk: If treatment could damage the fiber (e.g., bleach on wool), STOP. Explain to client, offer alternatives (professional re-dyeing, patching referral).
  • Bio-hazard contamination: If contamination has reached carpet padding or subfloor, recommend full extraction/replacement. Do not attempt surface-only treatment for deep contamination.
  • Client insists on treatment you advise against: Document the client's request and your recommendation in writing. Proceed only if no safety risk. If safety risk exists, refuse and explain why.
10

Close-Out

  1. Before/after photos uploaded to CRM
  2. Treatment details logged (chemical used, method, number of applications)
  3. Results documented (full removal, partial improvement, no change)
  4. Care instructions provided to client (verbal + written card)
  5. Follow-up reminder set if needed (7-day warranty check)
  6. Equipment cleaned, spotter kit restocked
