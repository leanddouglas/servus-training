01

Purpose

This SOP defines the standard workflow for completing a residential carpet cleaning job from dispatch through close-out. It ensures consistent service quality, professional client interactions, and accurate documentation for every residential appointment.

02

Scope

Applies to all standard residential carpet cleaning jobs including:

  • Single-family homes, townhouses, condos, apartments
  • Scheduled regular maintenance and one-time deep cleans
  • Rooms include: bedrooms, living rooms, hallways, stairs, basements
  • Excludes: commercial facilities, specialty restoration, flood damage (see separate SOPs)

Key Definitions:

  • Standard Clean — Hot Water Extraction (HWE) with pre-treatment
  • Light Soil — Regular maintenance, traffic lane refreshing
  • Heavy Soil — Deep embedded soil, pet areas, high-traffic zones requiring extra passes
  • Add-On Services — Scotchgard protector, deodorizer treatments, spot dyeing
03

Workflow Steps

1

Job Confirmation & Dispatch

Review job details in CRM/scheduling system (address, scope, special notes, access instructions).

Confirm appointment with client (text/call per company protocol) — ideally day before + morning of.

Load vehicle with required equipment and chemicals for the job scope.

Crew Leader Reviews job scope, confirms client, checks special instructions

Crew Member Loads equipment per checklist, verifies chemical stock levels

2

Arrival & Client Greeting

Arrive within scheduled window (±15 min).

Park professionally — do not block driveways or neighbor access.

Introduce team by name, present Servus ID/uniform.

Place door mat and corner guards before bringing in any equipment.

Crew Leader Greets client, introduces team, confirms scope of work

Crew Member Begins unloading equipment, places protective materials

3

Pre-Inspection Walk-Through

Walk every room in scope WITH the client.

Document existing conditions: stains, damage, wear patterns, furniture placement.

Take "before" photos of each area (minimum 2 per room — wide + close-up of concerns).

Set realistic expectations: "This stain may lighten but permanent dye damage cannot be fully removed."

Note any areas of concern (loose seams, delamination, pet damage).

Crew Leader Leads walk-through, documents conditions, manages client expectations

Crew Member Takes photos, notes furniture that needs moving

Safety: Flag trip hazards, loose carpet edges, or damaged subfloor before starting.
4

Equipment Setup

Position truckmount/portable unit — hose routing to minimize trip hazards.

Connect water supply (if truckmount).

Lay hose protectors at all doorways and transition areas.

Place "Wet Floor" signage at entry points.

Allow equipment to reach operating temperature before starting.

Crew Leader Oversees setup, verifies equipment is at operating specs

Crew Member Runs hoses, places protectors and signage, connects water

5

Pre-Vacuum & Furniture Prep

Move light furniture as agreed (lamps, small tables, dining chairs).

DO NOT move: heavy furniture, electronics, valuables, antiques — document and clean around.

Pre-vacuum all areas to be cleaned with CRI-approved commercial vacuum.

Pay special attention to edges, corners, and under furniture edges.

Crew Leader Confirms which furniture to move, handles fragile items

Crew Member Pre-vacuums all areas, moves agreed-upon furniture

6

Pre-Treatment Application

Apply pre-spray solution per FRP-004 (Pre-Treatment Application Procedure).

Adjust concentration based on soil level (light/standard/heavy — see chemical dilution chart).

Allow proper dwell time (5–10 min standard, up to 15 min for heavy soil).

Apply spot treatments to identified stains per FRP-009 (Spot vs Stain Identification).

Crew Leader Selects pre-treatment concentration, applies spot treatments

Crew Member Applies pre-spray in systematic pattern, monitors dwell time

7

HWE Extraction

Extract using hot water extraction per FRP-005 (HWE Extraction Procedure).

Maintain proper wand speed: 1 inch per second forward stroke.

Overlap passes by 50% for uniform coverage.

Use additional dry passes in high-moisture areas.

Water temperature: 150–200°F at the wand (per fiber type — reduce for olefin/polyester).

Monitor waste tank levels; empty as needed.

Crew Leader Monitors extraction quality, adjusts pressure/temperature

Crew Member Performs extraction in systematic room-by-room pattern

8

Post-Treatment & Drying Setup

Apply Scotchgard protector if purchased (while carpet is still damp).

Apply deodorizer treatment if purchased.

Set up air movers/fans for accelerated drying.

Place foam blocks or foil tabs under all furniture legs on damp carpet.

Estimated dry time: 4–8 hours (communicate to client).

Crew Leader Applies protector/deodorizer, sets client drying expectations

Crew Member Places blocks/tabs, positions air movers

9

Quality Inspection Walk-Through

Inspect every cleaned area under good lighting.

Check for: missed spots, wand marks, remaining stains, over-wetting.

Re-clean any areas that don't meet Servus quality standards.

Compare against "before" photos to verify improvement.

Crew Leader Performs quality inspection, authorizes re-cleaning if needed

Crew Member Re-cleans flagged areas

10

Client Sign-Off & Close-Out

Walk client through completed work room by room.

Point out results, especially where stains were treated.

Explain care instructions: drying time, when to replace furniture, avoid heavy traffic 24h.

Provide written care instructions card.

Collect client signature (digital or paper) confirming satisfaction.

Handle any concerns or complaints on-site if possible.

Crew Leader Leads final walk-through, collects sign-off, addresses concerns

Crew Member Begins equipment breakdown

11

Post-Job Equipment Care

Flush extraction lines with clean water.

Clean and inspect wand, hoses, and connectors.

Empty and rinse waste tank.

Restock chemical supplies for next job.

Complete post-job equipment checklist (per FRP-006).

Log job completion in CRM: notes, photos, any follow-up needed.

Crew Leader Reviews job log, ensures CRM is updated, files photos

Crew Member Cleans and restocks all equipment

04

Roles & Responsibilities

Role Key Responsibilities
Crew Leader Client communication, pre-inspection, quality decisions, documentation, CRM updates, sign-off collection
Crew Member Equipment setup/breakdown, pre-vacuum, extraction, furniture protection, equipment maintenance
Dispatch / Office Job scheduling, client confirmation calls, follow-up satisfaction surveys
05

Safety

  • Place "Wet Floor" signs at all entry points and damp areas
  • Use corner guards and hose protectors to prevent trip hazards
  • Wear PPE per WHMIS: gloves for chemical handling, eye protection for pre-spray mixing
  • Never leave running equipment unattended
  • Verify client's electrical outlets can handle equipment load before connecting
  • Report any property damage immediately to Crew Leader → Office
  • Follow WorkSafeBC ergonomic guidelines for lifting (max 50 lbs solo)
If a slip, trip, or fall occurs — document immediately, report to supervisor, complete incident form within 24 hours.
06

Systems & Tools

  • CRM/Scheduling: Job details, client history, appointment confirmation
  • Communication: Company phone for client texts/calls, internal team chat
  • Equipment: Truckmount or portable extractor, CRI-approved vacuum, air movers
  • Chemicals: Pre-spray, spot treatments, Scotchgard, deodorizer (approved product list)
  • Documentation: Digital photo capture (phone), inspection forms, care instruction cards
  • GPS/Navigation: For routing to job sites

08

Quality Checks

  • Pre-inspection photos taken (minimum 2 per room)
  • Pre-vacuum completed before any wet cleaning
  • Pre-treatment dwell time observed (5–15 min)
  • Extraction passes overlap by 50%
  • No visible wand marks or streaking
  • All identified stains treated and documented
  • Furniture protected with blocks/tabs
  • "Before vs. After" comparison confirms improvement
  • Client walk-through completed
  • Client sign-off collected
  • Post-job photos taken
  • CRM updated with notes and photos
09

Exceptions & Escalation

  • Client not home: Wait 10 min, attempt contact (call + text). If no response → contact Office for instructions. Do NOT enter without authorization.
  • Scope change on-site: Client requests additional rooms/services → Crew Leader quotes using price guide, gets verbal approval, documents in CRM.
  • Property damage discovered: Pre-existing damage → document with photos immediately. Damage caused by crew → report to Office within 1 hour, do not admit liability, complete incident form.
  • Equipment failure: Switch to backup unit if available. If no backup → contact Office to reschedule remaining work. Do not attempt repairs on-site that could delay the client.
  • Unsatisfied client: Attempt to resolve on-site (re-clean, additional treatment). If unresolvable → escalate to Office for follow-up within 24 hours.
  • Safety concern: Mold, asbestos suspicion, structural damage, pest infestation → STOP WORK. Notify client and Office immediately. Do not proceed until cleared.
STOP WORK situations: Mold, asbestos suspicion, structural damage, or pest infestation require immediate work stoppage and Office notification before any further action.
10

Close-Out

After every job, complete:

  1. Upload "before" and "after" photos to CRM
  2. Complete job notes: rooms cleaned, treatments applied, issues encountered
  3. Record time on-site (arrival → departure)
  4. Note any follow-up needed (re-clean, warranty claim, upsell opportunity)
  5. Equipment checklist completed and signed off
  6. Vehicle restocked for next job
